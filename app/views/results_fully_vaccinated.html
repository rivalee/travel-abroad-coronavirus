Returning to England

Because you said:

  • You are fully vaccinated
  • You are travelling with someone aged 0 to 4

Providing proof of vaccination

You must be able to prove that you've been fully vaccinated. If you live in England, you can prove your vaccination status using the NHS COVID Pass.

If you were vaccinated in another country or territory, see examples of what you can use as proof of vaccination.

Before you travel to England

  You must:

  • book and pay for a COVID-19 test - to be taken after arrival before the end of day 2
  • complete a passenger locator form - to be completed in the 48 hours before you arrive in England 
  • You can choose a lateral flow test or a PCR test. You must use a private test provider. You cannot use a free NHS test.
  • You will need to enter your COVID-19 test booking reference number on your passenger locator form.
  • You do not need to take a pre-departure test before returning to England if you're fully vaccinated.

When you arrive in England

You must take the COVID-19 test you booked before travel on or before day 2 (the day you arrive is day 0).

If the test result is negative, you do not need to self-isolate.

If a lateral flow test is positive, you must take a free PCR test to confirm the result. You must self-isolate until you get the result.

If a lateral flow test result is unclear, you must self-isolate for 10 full days (the day of the test is day 0).

If a PCR test is positive or unclear, you must self-isolate for 10 full days (the day of the test is day 0).

If either a lateral flow or PCR test is unclear, you can choose to take another private test.

Travelling to England with children aged 4 and under

Children aged 0 to 4 do not have to take any COVID-19 travel tests and do not have to quarantine on arrival in England.

Exemptions because of your job

Some of the rules for returning to England may not apply because of the job you do. Find out which jobs qualify for exemptions.

Arriving in England to receive urgent medical treatment

If you're arriving to receive urgent, unplanned treatment in England, you do not need to take any travel tests.

You can travel to and from your healthcare provider and the place you're staying in England and must follow quarantine rules.
